Muhammad Hakim Jaafar

 

Muhammad Hakim Jaafar (Hakim) is a central banker specialising in dealing with emerging issues. He serves as an analyst at Bank Negara Malaysia (Central Bank of Malaysia), with policy advisory and regulatory focus on payment systems, digital assets, sustainability and international relations. Hakim is an expert in the ASEAN financial regulation process. His experience spans the full-spectrum of central banking, from formulating strategies to advance regional financial integration, to advising policies for domestic financial stability and financial inclusion. This includes the interlinking of ASEAN payment systems; establishment of ASEAN Taxonomy Board; and co-founding of the central bank's Digital Currency Research Hub, leading the landscape review, developing the surveillance framework and assessing the impact of digital assets to financial stability and monetary stability. He has also published and co-authored several reports, notably the ASEAN Taxonomy for Sustainable Finance (Version 1), Report on the Roles of ASEAN Central Banks in Managing Climate and Environment-related Risks, and ASEAN Cybersecurity Resilience and Information Sharing Platform (only circulated amongst the ASEAN central bank community). Hakim holds a BSc (Hons) in Economics from the University of Warwick, under full scholarship by the central bank. He also had a stint at the University of Cambridge's Judge Business School, where he was the Research Lead for the Emergent Money Systems at the Cambridge Centre for Alternative Finance (CCAF). His work focused on the implications of digital assets to existing payment and monetary arrangements, which in turn could impact financial stability and monetary stability. Hakim is a Professional Fellow for the ASEAN-US Young Southeast Asian Leaders’ Initiative (YSEALI); and a Certified Climate Risk Professional from the Chartered Banker Institute.
